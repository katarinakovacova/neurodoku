Sudoku as a Prevention Against Dementia

Keeping the mind active is one of the most important ways to promote long-term brain health.

Sudoku challenges the brain with logic-based problem solving, which stimulates neural activity. This mental exercise helps maintain cognitive functions such as memory, reasoning, and attention.

Some studies suggest that people who regularly engage in puzzles like Sudoku may have a reduced risk of cognitive decline as they age.

While Sudoku is not a cure or guaranteed prevention, it is a fun and healthy habit that supports mental agility and alertness.
